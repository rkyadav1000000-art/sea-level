<h1>Sea Level</h1>
<body>
    <h2>Increasing Graph Satellite Observation</h2>
    <img width="550" height="230" src="sea level.graph.jpg" alt="Graph">
    <h4>Sea level rise refers to the increase in the average height of the world’s oceans. It’s one of the major impacts of
    climate change and is happening gradually—but with serious long-term effects.</h4>
    <h2>🌊 Why is sea level rising?</h2>
    <h4>The main causes are:</h4>
    <h3>1. Thermal expansion</h3>
    <h4>As ocean water warms, it expands. This is a key concept in Thermal Expansion.</h4>
    <h3>2. Melting ice</h3>
    <h4>* Glaciers and ice sheets (especially in Greenland and Antarctica) are melting faster.</h4>
    <h4>*  This adds more water to the oceans.</h4>
    <h3>3. Loss of polar ice sheets</h3>
    <h4>Large ice masses are shrinking due to rising global temperatures linked to Global Warming.</h4>
    <h2>📈 How fast is it rising?</h2>
    <h4>* Over the past century: about 15–20 cm (6–8 inches)</h4>
    <h4>* Current rate: about 3–4 mm per year, and accelerating</h4>
    <h2>⚠️ Why does it matter?</h2>
    <h4>Sea level rise can lead to:</h4>
    <h4>- Coastal flooding (especially during storms).</h4>
    <h4>- Erosion of beaches.</h4>
    <h4>- Saltwater intrusion into drinking water supplies.</h4>
    <h4>- Threats to major coastal cities like Mumbai, Kolkata, and New York City.</h4>
    <h4>Low-lying regions and island nations are the most vulnerable.</h4>
    <h2>🌍 What can be done?</h2>
    <h4>- Reducing greenhouse gas emissions.</h4>
    <h4>- Transitioning to renewable energy.</h4>
    <h4>- Coastal protection (like seawalls and mangroves).</h4>
    <h4>Better urban planning.</h4>
